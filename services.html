Platform Features for Waste Haulers

Municipal-contract capability from waste management routing software shows up in service-level tracking, contract reporting, resident-facing tools, and the route management for high-volume rigid routes. The list below covers what TackRoute delivers.

Core Feature Set

  • Route optimization with automatic stop sequencing and drag-and-drop reordering
  • Customer account management with service schedules, contact history, and contract terms
  • Automated recurring billing and invoicing with integrated payment processing
  • Customer self-service portal for schedule lookup, pickup requests, and payment updates
  • Driver mobile app with the day's route, customer notes, and proof-of-service capture
  • Real-time fleet dashboard with GPS truck tracking and route progress
  • Service tracking and missed-pickup workflow with audit trail
  • Container and roll-off inventory tracking for commercial and construction operations
  • Reporting and analytics on routes, revenue, aging receivables, and fleet performance
  • Integration points with QuickBooks and other accounting platforms
  • Multi-line-of-business support: residential curbside, commercial, roll-off, recycling, municipal

Route Optimization & Daily Route Planning

Route optimization for municipal-contract routes works against the contract specifications. Pickup days, time windows, and service-level requirements feed into the optimizer as constraints. The output is a route that complies with the contract while minimizing drive time. For municipalities with large residential bases, the optimization savings can be substantial — and the contract often allows the operator to capture the savings rather than rebate them.

Customer Accounts, Self-Service Portal & Communications

Customer accounts for municipal operations have two shapes. The municipal customer (the city or county) is the contract counterparty; the resident is the service user. The platform handles both — municipal customer record carries the contract, the resident roster carries the service population, and the customer portal serves residents directly. For municipalities with billing relationships to residents, the platform handles resident-level billing; for municipalities that pay the operator directly and bill residents themselves, the platform handles the municipal-side invoicing only.

Billing, Invoicing & Revenue Operations

Billing for municipal contracts is contract-driven rather than service-event-driven. The contract specifies the payment schedule; the platform tracks service performance against the contract; invoices follow the contract cadence. Any variable charges (overages, special services, extra-cycle work) are captured by service event and rolled into the appropriate invoice cycle. QuickBooks integration handles the general-ledger work.

Driver Mobile App, Dispatch & Fleet Visibility

Driver app for municipal routes supports the high-volume residential pattern. Drivers see the day's route on a phone or tablet, complete stops with timestamp and GPS, flag exceptions in structured form. For municipal routes with strict service-level requirements, the exception flagging is particularly important — missed pickups need to be captured immediately so the operator can schedule make-up runs within the contract's remediation window.

What Implementation Looks Like

Implementation for a municipal-contract operator runs the standard two-to-four-week pattern with extra attention to contract terms configuration. The contract's specific service-level definitions, reporting requirements, and remediation procedures all need to be reflected in the platform setup. Week 1: customer and resident roster import. Week 2: contract terms and reporting configuration. Week 3: shadow-running. Week 4: go-live.

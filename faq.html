Waste Hauler Software FAQ

Common questions waste collection companies ask before adopting hauler-focused software — answered plainly.

Does it support municipal-contract operations?

Yes. Municipal contracts are first-class operations on the platform. Service levels, reporting requirements, and remediation procedures all configure into the system to match the specific contract.

How does it handle reporting for a municipal customer?

Contract performance reports run on the cadence the contract specifies, with audit-grade data backing every metric. The reporting layer is the operator-facing contract-compliance tool.

Can it produce service-level reporting for compliance?

Yes. Service-level achievement tracks against contract specifications — missed pickup rates, exception remediation timelines, diversion targets where applicable. The reports satisfy the documentation requirements that municipal contracts typically attach.

Does it handle large volumes of residential stops on rigid routes?

Yes. High-volume residential routes on contract-specified pickup days are supported. The optimizer produces compliant routes while minimizing drive time within the contract's constraints.

How are route-level KPIs tracked?

Per-route completion rate, on-time percentage, exception frequency, miles and time tracking, and the leading indicators that flag performance issues before they show up in monthly reporting.

Can the customer portal serve a municipal end-resident base?

Yes. The portal points at the resident base — schedule lookup, pickup requests, basic service information. The operator-branded experience scales to the municipality's resident population without office overhead growth.

How does it handle peak-season volume?

Volume variability (holiday weeks, weather events, special collections) absorbs into the scheduling layer. Routes can be expanded or split for peak periods without breaking the underlying contract structure.

Does it manage the bid-and-renewal data cycle for contracts?

Reporting produces the per-route, per-territory, and per-service-line data needed for contract bidding and renewal negotiations. The operator's contract decisions get informed by structured data rather than aggregate guesses.
